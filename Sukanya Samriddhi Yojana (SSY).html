Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

A special savings scheme for the girl child

What is Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana?

The Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) is a government-backed savings scheme aimed at securing the future of the girl child. Launched as part of the 'Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao' campaign, SSY offers an attractive interest rate and tax benefits. It is designed to encourage parents to invest for the welfare and future education of their daughters.

Benefits of SSY

  • High Interest Rate: Offers one of the highest interest rates among savings schemes.
  • Tax Benefits: Contributions are eligible for tax deductions under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.
  • Security: Government-backed scheme ensuring the safety of the principal amount.
  • Flexible Investment Amount: Minimum ₹250 and maximum ₹1.5 lakh per year.
  • Withdrawal Facility: Partial withdrawal allowed after the girl turns 18 years old for her education.

Eligibility Criteria

The scheme is available for:

  • Parents or guardians of a girl child below 10 years of age.
  • There is no restriction on the number of accounts a person can open, but the maximum number of accounts per family is two.

How Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana Works?

To open an account, parents or guardians need to approach the nearest post office or authorized bank and provide necessary documents. The account can be opened with a minimum deposit of ₹250, and the investment can go up to ₹1.5 lakh annually.

Interest is compounded annually and credited to the account. The account matures when the girl turns 21 years old, and the corpus can be used for higher education or marriage expenses.

Contact Information

If you have any questions or need more information, you can visit your nearest Post Office or Bank, or visit the official government website.
