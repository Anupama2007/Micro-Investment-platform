National Savings Certificate (NSC)

Your Secure and Safe Investment Option

What is NSC?

The National Savings Certificate (NSC) is a savings bond offered by the Indian government that provides tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. It is a fixed-income investment scheme with a guaranteed return and is considered a safe investment option for investors.

Key Features

  • Government-backed, low-risk investment
  • Tax benefits under Section 80C
  • Fixed interest rate
  • Available in denominations of ₹100, ₹500, ₹1,000, ₹5,000, ₹10,000
  • Interest paid at maturity
  • Eligibility: Available to individuals, minors, and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs)

Eligibility

The National Savings Certificate (NSC) can be purchased by:

  • Indian citizens (HUFs, minors, and adults)
  • Joint accounts (two adults)

Interest Rates

The interest rate for NSC is updated quarterly by the Government of India. For the current financial year, the interest rate is around 6.8% p.a. compounded annually.

Investment Details

NSC is available in the following denominations:

  • ₹100
  • ₹500
  • ₹1,000
  • ₹5,000
  • ₹10,000

Minimum investment is ₹100, and there is no maximum limit for the total investment. The investment tenure is 5 years, and the interest earned is payable only at maturity.

Tax Benefits

Investments in NSC qualify for tax deductions under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, with a maximum deduction limit of ₹1.5 lakh per annum. The interest earned on the NSC is taxable, but it can be claimed as a deduction under Section 80C if reinvested annually.
