About Fixed Deposits (FDs)

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a financial instrument provided by banks and financial institutions that offers a higher interest rate than a regular savings account, until the given maturity date.

FDs are one of the safest investment options, offering guaranteed returns. The principal amount is safe and returns are predetermined, making them suitable for risk-averse investors.

Benefits of Fixed Deposits

  • Safety and Security: FD is a low-risk investment option with guaranteed returns.
  • Fixed Interest Rate: Enjoy a fixed interest rate, ensuring predictable returns.
  • Flexible Tenure: You can choose the tenure, ranging from 7 days to 10 years.
  • Tax Benefits: Tax-saving Fixed Deposits come with benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.
  • Easy to Open: FDs can be easily opened with minimal documentation.

How Fixed Deposits Work

To open a Fixed Deposit, you need to deposit a lump sum amount with a bank or financial institution. The amount will remain locked for the tenure you choose. The interest will either be paid periodically or accumulated with the principal amount at maturity.

At the end of the tenure, you can withdraw the principal amount along with the accrued interest. The longer the tenure, the higher the interest rate in most cases.
