What are Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)?

Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs) are government securities issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) on behalf of the Indian government. They are an excellent investment option for those looking to invest in gold without physically purchasing gold. The bonds are denominated in grams of gold and offer interest and potential capital appreciation.

Benefits of SGB

  • Safe Investment: Backed by the Government of India, making them a secure investment option.
  • Interest Income: Earn an annual interest of 2.5% on the amount invested, paid every six months.
  • Capital Appreciation: The price of the bond is linked to the market price of gold, offering potential returns based on gold price increases.
  • Tax Benefits: No capital gains tax is charged if bonds are held until maturity (8 years).
  • Convenience: Invest in gold without the need for physical storage or security concerns.

Eligibility:

Both Indian residents and institutions are eligible to invest in SGBs. The minimum investment is 1 gram, and the maximum limit is 4 kg for individuals, 20 kg for HUFs, and 20 kg for trusts.

Key Features of Sovereign Gold Bonds

Interest Rate

2.5% annual interest, paid every 6 months on the initial investment value.

Tenure

The bond tenure is 8 years, with an exit option available after 5 years.

Gold Link

The value of the bond is linked to the price of gold in the market, providing an opportunity for capital appreciation.

Taxation

No capital gains tax if the bond is held until maturity (8 years). Tax is also exempt on the interest earned during the tenure.
